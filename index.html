A plastic pollution project

What is Plastic Pollution?

Plastic pollution is a build-up of synthetic plastic products that negatively affect natural habitats for humans and wildlife. Plastic pollution is one of the most significant contributors to climate change. Plastic is nearly impossible to break down, winds up in landfills, and the ocean where it adversely affects marine life. Additionally, waste management cannot effectively recycle most plastic (even those with a recycling symbol), which means that most plastic waste ends up in landfills or the ocean.

It's important to regularly step back and think about how our everyday actions impact the planet. Right now, there are 150 million metric tons of plastic in our oceans - and we're releasing an additonal 8 million metric tons each year. Plastic pollution exists almost everywhere in the ocean, from the remote seas of the Artic to the floor of the deep sea.

Did You Know?

Enough plastic is thrown away each year to circle the earth four times.

50 percent of the plastic we use, we use just once and throw away.

Americans throw away 35 billion plastic water bottles every year.

It takes 500-1,000 years for plastic to degrade.

The equivalent of a truckload of plastic enters the oceans every minute.

Only 1 percent of plastic bags are returned for recycling.

Plastic is killing more than 1.1 million seabirds and animals every year.

When plastic breaks down, it turns into smaller pieces of plastic known as microplastics.

A single American throws away 185 pounds of plastic annually, approximately half of which is single-use.

Where Can You Start?

Here are 5 ways you can start rising above plastic!

Seek out alternatives to the plastic items that you rely on

Bring your to-go mug with you to the coffee shop

Carry reusable utensils in your purse, backpack or car

Reframe from purchasing single-use plastics

Spread the word. Talk to your family and friends
